"Neither LoC nor PRs alone fully captures an engineer's true impact on the team. While PRs provide an objective metric that reflects the quantity of contribution, we need to identify the ones that were truly effective, not abandoned or eventually reverted. Engineers should only be credited for PRs whose implementations lasted in production. In addition, PRs vary in complexity, but what truly separates PRs is their ability to enable or unblock other team members, creating a team-multiplying effect. When an engineer credits another engineer's PR, the downstream effect is attributed to the original engineer who made the PR possible. Collectively, the sum of effective and enabled PRs provides an intuitive and efficient metric to measure the full impact of quantity, quality, and reach. From an implementation standpoint, this data parsing traversal is also especially fast, replicable, and scalable."

Wesley Lin
